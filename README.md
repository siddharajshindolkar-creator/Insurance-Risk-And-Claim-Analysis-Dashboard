***Insurance Risk & Claim Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)***

**An interactive Power BI dashboard built to analyze insurance risk and claim patterns across customer demographics, vehicle attributes, and geographic regions—providing actionable insights into claim behavior, risk exposure, and business performance**



***1. Project Overview:***

Understanding insurance risk and claim behavior is essential for minimizing losses and optimizing pricing strategies. This project focuses on analyzing insurance data to uncover:

* Claim trends across customer segments
* Risk distribution by geography and demographics
* High-risk vehicle types and usage patterns
* Claim frequency and severity insights

The dashboard enables **data-driven decision-making** for underwriting, risk management, and business strategy teams.



***2. Objectives:***

* Track total policies and claim amount
* Analyze claim frequency and average claim value
* Identify high-risk customer segments
* Evaluate claim distribution by car type and usage
* Understand impact of demographics (age, education, marital status)
* Analyze geographic risk patterns



***3. Dashboard Highlights:***

**Key Metrics**
* Total Policies: 37,542
* Total Claim Amount: $187.8M
* Overall Claim Frequency: 0.5
* Average Claim Amount: $5.0K
* Gender Distribution: Male (18.7K), Female (18.8K)


**Key Visuals**

*Total Claim Amount by Car Use (Donut Chart)*
* Compares claim contribution between private and commercial vehicles

*Total Claim Amount by Car Make (Bar Chart)*
* Highlights high-risk vehicle brands such as Ford and Chevrolet

*Total Claim Amount by Coverage Zone (Donut Chart)*
* Shows claim distribution across urban, suburban, and rural areas

*Total Claim Amount by Age Group (Bar Chart)*
* Identifies high-risk age segments (26–55 years)

*Total Claim Amount by Car Year (Line Chart)*
* Displays trends in claim amounts over time

*Total Claim Amount by Kids Driving (Area Chart)*
* Analyzes impact of number of kids on claim behavior

*Total Claim Amount by Education (Donut Chart)*
* Shows claim distribution across education levels

*Claim Amount by Education & Marital Status (Matrix)*
* Provides deeper segmentation of customer behavior


**Filters (Slicers)**

* Claim Type / Metric selector
* Interactive filtering across visuals


**Key Insights**

* Urban areas contribute the highest claim amounts, indicating higher risk exposure
* Mid-age group (26–55) shows the highest claim frequency and value
* Certain car brands dominate claim costs, suggesting exposure concentration
* Private vehicle usage contributes significantly more to total claims
* Claim trends show growth over time with potential recent data drop
* Singles and bachelor-level customers contribute higher claim amounts



***4. Tech Stack:***
